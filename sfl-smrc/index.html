SFLMFGrowth supports advanced and emerging manufacturers across Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties — strengthening regional supply chains, competitiveness, and resilience through an open and transparent process.

Overview

A growth platform for South Florida manufacturers

SFLMFGrowth helps manufacturers modernize operations, access capital, develop talent, and reach new markets — coordinating industry, academic, and government partners around shared regional goals.

The initiative provides comprehensive support to small and mid-sized manufacturers, with a focus on digital transformation, ERP and AI adoption, investment readiness, and export development. Participation is open to eligible manufacturers across the tri-county region.

Program Pillars

Four pillars of manufacturing growth

Each pillar offers structured support designed to strengthen competitiveness and long-term resilience.

Pillar 01

Technical Assistance & Digital Transformation

One-on-one coaching to implement ERP systems, AI-driven analytics, and smart manufacturing tools, plus bootcamps and training.

Pillar 02

Capital Access & Investment Readiness

Financial modeling, investor-ready data packs, and connections to funding sources to support expansion and modernization.

Pillar 03

Workforce Development & Talent

Innovation internships and training pathways that connect manufacturers with emerging talent and a skilled workforce.

Pillar 04

Market Expansion & Export Readiness

Export assistance and market development, with a focus on Latin America and the Caribbean alongside broader global markets.

Pillar 05

Innovation & Commercialization

Support for SBIR/STTR pathways, patent development, and bringing innovative products from concept to market.

Pillar 06

Supply Chain Resilience

Strengthening regional supply chains and operational resilience to help manufacturers adapt and compete.

Open Calls & Procurement

An open and transparent process

SFLMFGrowth will use an open and transparent process for manufacturer participation and prospective contractor support. Manufacturers will be invited to apply through an open call, and prospective vendors, consultants, and service providers will be considered through an appropriate open process consistent with applicable grant requirements.
